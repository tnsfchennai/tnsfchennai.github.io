About Program







This is part of its efforts to popularize science to the General Public and Students who are pursuing science as their career. TNSF attempt to focus on students on higher science as everyone knows that learning of science at college within the curriculum is not enough to acquire holistic knowledge of science at the appropriate time. Hence, to fill the gap between what students are acquiring through the curriculum and what it is required, TNSF is planning its activities on higher science to students who are pursuing higher education.



This lecture will cover the unknown aspects of Ramanujan for the common audience. This lecture starts with a simple illustration of one of the important contribution of Ramanujan to the world of mathematics and explains what is 'mathematical thinking'. The way Ramanujan has been able to 'make friends with numbers' is lore, often associated with mysticism. How did Ramanujan achieve it? What does his life stories tell us about them? The lecture will also draw the attention of the audience to the not so much spoken features of his life, his emotional suffering, and how it perhaps led him to depression and attempted suicide. Although celebrated today, how the Indian society treated him, while he was alive needs to be remembered so that we don't repeat those atrocities. The last part will illustrate how some of his work is now being re-discovered and found to be handy to understand the extreme condiction of the black hole, where the known physics breaks down, as well as more mundane practical applications like cryptography.

Speakers

Dr. T.V. Venkateswaran

Dr. T.V. Venkateswaran is Scientist (F) with ‘Vigyan Prasar’, National Institute for Science Communication, Department of Science and Technology, New Delhi.

He is the founder chief editor of “India Science Wire” an online science and technology news feature service published by the Department of Science and Technology.

In association with Rajya Saba TV (RSTV), he conducts a widely watched television weekly show -Eureka-Conversation with an Indian scientist for the past four years.

He is popular in Tamulnadu for his weekly question and answer series in PATTAM, a supplement of DINAMALAR and weekly column on contemporary developments in S&T in The Tamil-Hindu and a regular contributor to various journals and magazines on popular science and showcasing scientific activities in Indian labs and research Institutions. He has published several articles and more than 50 popular science books, in Tamil and Hindi. Some of them have been translated into Hindi, Malayalam, Kannada and Telugu. He regularly writes on scientific topics in the Federal, Ippothu.com, Frontline, Down to Earth, Science Reporter, Indradanush, Chakmak, Sandarp and other periodicals.

His research work is in the area of science, culture and media. .He has contributed an article to the compilation “Bridging the Communication Gap in Science and Technology. Lessons from India. Editors: Bagla, Pallava, Binoy, V. V. His research interest includes history of science popularization in Tamil nadu and critically examining the part played by science communication in fabrication of varied worldviews, in particular in Indian cultural cosmos. He is currently engaged in investigating the history if astronomy in Tamil region, especially the evolution, reception, spread of vakya panchanga and calendrical system since medieval period. His R&D profile includes development of science communication software as well as innovative strategies for science education and informal learning. His most recent paper is "Heavenly Bodies, Celestial Phenomena and Calendrical Data in Tamil Epigraphical Inscriptions (Fifteenth to Seventeenth Centuries)." NTM (2020)

He is also the founder member of the Indian Scientists Response to COVID19, a network of more than 500 Indian scientists from various disciplines, across institutions, from all over the world. The activities can be accessed https://www.indscicov.in
