About Neutrino Physics

Neutrinos are elementary particles in the sense that are indivisible unlike atoms. In 1930 Pauli first postulated the existence of particles called neutrinos. Its existence was first proved in 1959 . Neutrinos are produced in our body, in the interior of the earth, in our atmosphere, in the interior of our sun, in the Supernova Explosion.

Detecting a neutrino particle is a big challenge since it practically does not interact with matter. It can go through our body or earth or interstellar matter without being unaffected. But, If we detect them it gives a vast wealth of information especially events in deep past and deep space. Neutrinos are very good messengers from distant past and distant space. It can give information about early Universe, Black hole, neutron star, interior of the Sun. Properties of neutrinos come with rich physics.



Neutrinos were originally supposed to be massless particles. But recent evidences suggest they have very small mass leading to interesting phenomena. They come in three distinct types ( or flavors as known in particle physics). Electron neutrino, muon neutrino, tau neutrino.

The Standard Model (The most successful model in Theoretical Physics describing the nature of elementary particles) contains a very interesting phenomenon called Neutrino Oscillation. Neutrinos can change their identities. A neutrino which starts as electron neutrino can become muon neutrino or a tau neutrino as it traverses towards earth. The identity of a neutrino keeps on changing as it traverses from the Sun to the Earth. ( You can imagine a scenario like vehicle constantly changing its identity as a car or van or a truck. It is similar to constant changing of the identity of an animal from tiger to cheetah to a cat as the animal travels).

Speakers

Dr. Vivek Murlidhar Datar

Dr. Vivek Murlidhar Datar, Project Director, India based Neutrino Observatory (INO) Project and Head INO Cell, TIFR (since 1 May, 2015). He served as Head, Nuclear Physics Division, Bhabha Atomic Research Centre (BARC) (Nov 2012 - April 2015). He received Outstanding Scientist (OS) in Sept.2009 and Distinguished Scientist (DS) March 2015. He is part of Dept. of Atomic Energy and Senior Professor (since Sept 2009), Homi Bhabha National Institute (HBNI), a deemed-to-be University.


Dr. T.R.Govindarajan

Dr. T.R.Govindarajan, fondly called TRG in academic circles is having more than 35 years of experience in academics and research. His area of specialization is High Energy Physics and he did research on Gravitational force. He is an Adjunct Professor in Chennai Mathematical Institute. He is also an emeritus Professor in Institute of Mathematical Sciences. He has also worked as a professor in Chennai Loyola College. He visited various high profile research institutions functioning in various part of the world that include Max Plank Institute. He has published more than 90 research papers.


Dr. Indumathi D

Dr. D. Indumathi is Professor, The Institute of Mathematical Sciences, Chennai. Her area of interest is in High energy physics phenomenology and particularly Neutrinos from solar, atmospheric and stellar sources, effects of mixing and oscillation; decay, Nucleon and nuclear structure functions, including polarised structure functions, QED at finite temperature,cInclusive hadroproduction at colliders.


Dr. Deepak Samuel

Dr. Deepak Samuel, Assistant Professor, Department of Physics, Central University of Karnataka since 2014. Postdoctoral fellowship in the field of proton therapy designing a novel system for range verification in prostate treatments at Universite Catholique de Louvain, Belgium/Harvard Medical School, Boston during 2012-2014. He was Project Scientist and worked for the India-based Neutrino collaboration in simulations and data Analysis at Tata Institute of Fundamental Research, Mumbai during 2011-2012. Visiting Fellow Worked for the India-based Neutrino Observatory (INO) collaboration in designing a fast VME-based data acquisition system for a cosmic muon telescope at Tata Institute of Fundamental Research, Mumbai 2008-2011. He was also involved in various outreach programs held in Tamil Nadu to create awareness about the project.
